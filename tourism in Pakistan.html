K2, the second-highest mountain in the world, is located in the Karakoram Range. These mountains play an important role in climate, water resources, and tourism.

Landscapes of Pakistan

Pakistan has diverse landscapes, including fertile plains, rugged mountains, coastal areas, and plateaus. The Indus Plain is one of the most fertile regions, supporting agriculture. The Balochistan Plateau features dry and rocky land, while the coastal areas along the Arabian Sea are rich in marine life.

Lakes of Pakistan

Pakistan has many beautiful natural and artificial lakes. Saif-ul-Malook Lake in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa is famous for its scenic beauty. Attabad Lake in Gilgit-Baltistan was formed due to a landslide and attracts many tourists. Manchar Lake in Sindh is one of the largest freshwater lakes in South Asia.

Deserts

Pakistan has several deserts, mostly located in the southern and eastern regions. The Thar Desert is the largest desert, spreading across Sindh and Punjab. The Cholistan Desert lies in southern Punjab, while the Kharan Desert is found in Balochistan. These deserts have extreme climates and unique wildlife.

Cities

Pakistan has many important cities that serve as cultural, economic, and political centers. Islamabad is the capital city, known for its planned layout. Karachi is the largest city and the economic hub of the country. Lahore is famous for its historical sites and culture, while Peshawar and Quetta are important cities in the northwest and southwest regions.
